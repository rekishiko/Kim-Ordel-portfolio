Training

My journey to becoming a software engineer began in Spring 2024 when I made the decision to pursue a career that would challenge me intellectually and allow me to collaborate with others to create innovative products. I began with self-study, first focusing on Python and then expanding to other topics such as AI, Java, HTML and Agile. While I quickly discovered a passion for coding, I realized I needed structure to guide my learning so I enrolled in the University of the People's Computer Science bachelor's program. This decision has provided me structure and guidance, allowing me to advance my skills and encounter topics I would not have otherwise.

Since deciding to become a software engineer, I have learned from various resources and remain committed to continuous learning. I believe that in this field, as in many others, staying current with industry changes and consistently bettering ourselves is crucial for both professional growth and achieving success.

This page serves as a detailed overview of my training journey, highlighting my dedication and the skills I have acquired. I believe that every learning opportunity, regardless of its size, is a chance to grow professionally and personally. My belief in self-improvement is guided by my grandfather’s optimism. Whenever there was an achievement my grandfather always said “another success,” a reminder that no matter how small, each achievement is a success that builds upon the last.

Sololearn is a program available on mobile and desktop that offers courses in programming languages, web development and AI. It focuses on short lessons, quizzes and hands-on practice. I used this when I started out and did not know any coding yet.

Simplilearn is a training program available online and through an app that offers training in soft and hard skills. The focus is on trianing to prepare for certifications and explore topics that have certifications to see if you want to progress in seeking the certification.

Qureca focuses on providing professional resources to teach about skills for the quantum workforce. I took some of the free courses as part of the training for my internship with Piedra Alta Sistemas
