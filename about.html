About

I’m Kim Ordel, a software engineer with a background in education. My journey from the humanities to a career in teaching and now to software engineering has given me a unique perspective. My academic background, which includes a bachelor's from San Diego State University and a master’s from the University of Southern California, taught me a crucial lesson: I prefer collaborating on creative projects over independent research. Through my studies I gained skills in research, analysis, and structuring complex information, all of which I apply to master new technologies and solve problems.

My career in teaching, tutoring, and special education gave me a versatile skill set, including credentials in social studies, Japanese, and foundational mathematics. My experience with students who have diverse needs has taught me to see challenges from new perspectives and to find adaptive solutions. I have honed my ability to solve complex problems and communicate effectively by adapting to diverse needs and seeing challenges from new perspectives.

My passion for learning led me to seek out new experiences, including a year studying abroad at Yokohama National University. Living on my own for the first time in my life I not only had to adapt to a new culture but learn life skills. My experiences abroad deepened my skills in problem-solving and has become a foundational part of how I approach new challenges today.

My mission is to apply these skills as an educator and problem-solver to develop innovative solutions that can make a meaningful impact on the world.
